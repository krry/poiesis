** POIESIS **

Bring your verses to life

# The Flow

The user is called the Poet. The Poet offers a poem. They begin at the composer interface that draws heavily on iA Writer and its syntax highlighting for parts of speech and beautiful duo-spaced typography. We'll default to monospaced fonts like Inconsolata, Fira Code, Fantasque Sans Mono, Recursive.

The app is called Poiesis. Poiesis receives the poem and channels it three ways: to the Editor, the Illustator, and the Narrator, which all feeds back to the Curator.

The Editor is a language model that specializes in polishing poetry. It looks at meter, rhythm, word choice, imagery, natural rhyme, metaphor, and many aspects of the submitted poem as a professional editor of a poetry journal would. It draws on vast collections of recent poetry, and lets the poet specify inspirations and styles by name and genre. The Editor produces revision suggestions, image references, craft observations, and emotional stage notes (for the narrator) as inline annotations and popovers. If the poet has automatic mode turned on, the Illustrator and narrator receive the revised, annotated poem and begin their work. Otherwise the poet may review the updates and then manually initiate the next phase.

The Illustrator takes these revisions and distills them into prompts for the image generator, grouping its prompts into evocative images, by line or stanza depending on the density, length, and variety of the content. It tunes the image prompts for the poet's choice of image generator, displays the prompts, and queues and sends them as requests for images.

The Narrator receives the revised and annotated poem and, depending on the chosen text-to-speech channel, packages and sends it off for narration generation.

As the images and audio return, the Curator receives them along with the revised poem and its annotations. The Curator packages all this into an interactive swipeable reading of the poem. The images as fullscreen background, the relevant lines of the poem as beautifully typed captions and overlays, and the narration playing synchronously. The curated poiesis is available for export as a mp4, as a share to TikTok or instagram, or as a shareable link to the Poiesis site itself.
